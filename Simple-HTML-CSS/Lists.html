Genres of Anime

Isekai:-

Isekai is a genre of anime in which the main characters enter a new world unlike their own, typically resembling an MMORPG or fantasy land.

Psychological:-

Where the main focus of the anime is: the mental state of the characters; the effect that the events taking place have on the character's minds; mental disorders such as anxiety, depression, or an existential crisis; abstract, mentally-taxing mindgames.

Harem:-

A Genre characterized by a central male protagonist who is typically surrounded by a group of female characters, each of whom may have romantic or affectionate feelings for him.

Slice of Life:-

Focuses on portraying everyday life and ordinary experiences of characters, often without dramatic or fantastical elements

Seinen:-

Targeted at adult male audiences. These anime series often feature more mature and complex themes, including violence, sexuality, psychological elements, and social commentary.

Shonen:-

Aimed at a young male audience, typically teenagers. These anime series often feature themes of adventure, action, friendship, and coming-of-age

Shoujo:-

aimed at a young female audience, typically teenagers. These anime series often revolve around themes of romance, relationships, emotions, and personal growth.
